Deeper Look at the <big> Tag

The <big> tag was once a simple way to make text appear larger than the default. It increases the font size by one level, but it is now considered outdated and replaced by CSS for better control.

Why It Was Used:

  • Easy and quick way to enlarge a word or sentence without writing extra styles
  • Supported in early HTML versions like HTML 3.2 and 4.01
  • Often used in headings, labels, or to highlight key content
